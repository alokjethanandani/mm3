founder's story

My family immigrated to America when I was eleven years old. To me, America is a concept and a country. America allows you to be the best version of you. The only thing barring you from pursuing your dreams is yourself.

In my personal and professional life, I look to great masters in art and science for inspiration. Leonardo da Vinci’s philosophy was that nature “expressed her purpose in the structure,” and it was precisely this observation that drove him to be exact and turned him towards mathematics, and a passion for the actual, which urged him to experiment. Leonardo Da Vinci made real the feeling that every individual carries in him or her unlimited potential and requires, not a lifetime of monastic devotion to one kind of work, but simply the proper environment in which he or she can unfold.

As an entrepreneur, I am on the lookout for big ideas that change the world. Which is why I recommend reading "Abundance" by Peter Diamandis, one of my favorite books. Within the book's pages, you’ll find out how to save the world in the coming era of abundance that will replace eons of scarcity. It's that sort of thinking where you can see how I look toward tomorrow for opportunities.

While I was born in Vancouver, I grew up in a small town close to New Delhi, India. I recall fondly learning to play Cricket and spending summers with my grandparents in the scenic north Indian town of Dehardun. My family eventually settled in sunny California, where I decided to attend UCLA (go Bruins!), earning a B.A. in Design.

When I’m not working, I enjoy traveling. Next on my list is Morocco, Egypt and Turkey.
